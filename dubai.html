Dubai

Future Skyline, Golden Deserts, and Unmatched Luxury

Overview

Dubai is a city of superlatives, where the world's tallest buildings rise from the Arabian desert. A global hub for shopping and innovation, it offers a unique blend of traditional Bedouin heritage and a ultra-modern lifestyle that feels years ahead of its time.

Best Time November — March
Currency UAE Dirham (AED)
Language Arabic & English

Must Visit

  • Burj Khalifa: The world's tallest building, offering an observation deck with views that stretch across the Gulf.
  • Palm Jumeirah: A man-made island shaped like a palm tree, home to some of the world's most luxurious resorts.
  • The Dubai Mall: More than just shopping, it features a massive aquarium, an indoor ice rink, and the famous Dubai Fountain.
  • Old Dubai & Souks: Take an abra across the creek to explore the historic Gold and Spice souks for a taste of traditional trade.

The Taste of Dubai

Try traditional Majboos (spiced rice with meat), enjoy Luqaimat (sweet dumplings with date syrup), and experience a luxury Desert Safari Dinner under the stars.
