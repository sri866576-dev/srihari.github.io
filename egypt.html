Egypt

Land of Pharaohs, Golden Sands, and Eternal History

Overview

Egypt is a country linking northeast Africa with the Middle East, dating to the time of the pharaohs. Millennia-old monuments sit along the fertile Nile River Valley, including Giza's colossal Pyramids and Great Sphinx as well as Luxor's hieroglyph-lined Karnak Temple and Valley of the Kings tombs.

Best Time October — April
Currency Egyptian Pound (EGP)
Language Arabic

Must Visit

  • Great Pyramids of Giza: The last remaining Wonder of the Ancient World, guarded by the enigmatic Great Sphinx.
  • Valley of the Kings: A royal burial ground in Luxor containing over 60 tombs, including that of Tutankhamun.
  • Abu Simbel: Two massive rock temples in southern Egypt, carved out of the mountainside during the reign of Ramesses II.
  • Khan el-Khalili: A famous bazaar and souq in the historic center of Cairo, offering a sensory journey through spices and crafts.

The Taste of Egypt

Experience the rich flavors of Egypt with dishes like koshari, molokhia, and fresh seafood from the Red Sea.
