Iceland

Glacial Lagoons, Volcanic Plains, and Celestial Lights

Overview

Iceland is a Nordic island nation defined by its dramatic landscape with volcanoes, geysers, hot springs, and lava fields. Massive glaciers are protected in Vatnajökull and Snæfellsjökull national parks. Most of the population lives in the capital, Reykjavik, which runs on geothermal power.

Best Time Sept — March (Aurora)
Currency Icelandic Króna (ISK)
Language Icelandic

Must Visit

  • The Blue Lagoon: A geothermal spa located in a lava field, known for its mineral-rich, milky-blue waters.
  • Golden Circle: A popular route featuring the Þingvellir National Park, the Geysir Geothermal Area, and Gullfoss Waterfall.
  • Jökulsárlón: A massive glacial lagoon filled with icebergs, located on the edge of Vatnajökull National Park.
  • Skógafoss: One of the biggest waterfalls in the country, with a drop of 60 meters and a width of 25 meters.

The Taste of Iceland

Try the famous Icelandic Hot Dog (Pylsur), enjoy fresh Atlantic Salmon, and experience the unique texture of Skyr, a traditional thick dairy product similar to yogurt.
