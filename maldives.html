Maldives

Turquoise Lagoons, Coral Atolls, and Absolute Seclusion

Overview

The Maldives is a tropical nation in the Indian Ocean composed of 26 ring-shaped atolls, which are made up of more than 1,000 coral islands. It’s known for its extensive reefs, blue lagoons, and white-sand beaches, making it the world's premier destination for luxury overwater living.

Best Time November — April
Currency Maldivian Rufiyaa (MVR)
Language Dhivehi & English

Must Visit

  • Male City: The densely populated capital known for its colorful buildings, historic Friday Mosque, and bustling fish markets.
  • Vaadhoo Island: Famous for the "Sea of Stars," where bioluminescent plankton makes the shoreline glow bright blue at night.
  • Banana Reef: One of the oldest dive sites in the Maldives, offering incredible coral formations and diverse marine life.
  • Hanifaru Bay: A UNESCO Biosphere Reserve where you can snorkel with hundreds of manta rays and whale sharks.

The Taste of Maldives

Try the traditional breakfast Mas Huni (tuna, coconut, and chili), enjoy fresh Grilled Lobster on a sandbank, and sip on Kurumba (fresh coconut water) while watching the sunset.
