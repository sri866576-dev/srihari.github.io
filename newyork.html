New York

The City That Never Sleeps: Energy, Art, and Ambition

Overview

New York City is a global powerhouse of culture, finance, and entertainment. Comprising five boroughs sitting where the Hudson River meets the Atlantic Ocean, its iconic skyline is defined by skyscrapers like the Empire State Building and the massive, sprawling Central Park.

Best Time Sept — Nov / May — June
Currency US Dollar ($)
Language English

Must Visit

  • Times Square: The bright, neon-lit heart of the theater district and the site of the famous New Year's Eve ball drop.
  • Central Park: A 843-acre oasis in the middle of Manhattan, featuring lakes, theaters, and the famous Bethesda Terrace.
  • Statue of Liberty: An enduring symbol of freedom located on Liberty Island, accessible by ferry from Battery Park.
  • Brooklyn Bridge: A historic suspension bridge offering the best walking path for views of the Manhattan skyline.

The Taste of New York

Grab a classic New York Style Pizza Slice for a dollar, enjoy a Street Halal Platter, or visit a traditional Jewish deli for a massive Pastrami on Rye.
