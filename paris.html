Paris

The City of Light, Art, and Timeless Romance

Overview

Paris is a global center for art, fashion, and culture. Its 19th-century cityscape is crisscrossed by wide boulevards and the River Seine. Beyond landmarks like the Eiffel Tower, the city is known for its cafe culture and designer boutiques along the Rue du Faubourg Saint-Honoré.

Best Time June — August
Currency Euro (€)
Language French

Must Visit

  • Eiffel Tower: The iconic iron lattice tower on the Champ de Mars, offering panoramic views of the entire city.
  • The Louvre: The world's largest art museum and a historic monument, home to the Mona Lisa and Venus de Milo.
  • Montmartre: A large hill in Paris's 18th arrondissement, known for its artistic history and the white-domed Basilica of the Sacré-Cœur.
  • Notre-Dame Cathedral: A medieval Catholic cathedral on the Île de la Cité, considered one of the finest examples of French Gothic architecture.

The Taste of Paris

Indulge in a buttery Croissant from a local boulangerie, enjoy colorful Macarons from Ladurée, and savor a classic Steak Frites at a traditional Parisian bistro.
