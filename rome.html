Rome

The Eternal City: Where Empire Meets Renaissance

Overview

Rome is a sprawling, cosmopolitan city with nearly 3,000 years of globally influential art, architecture, and culture on display. Ancient ruins such as the Forum and the Colosseum evoke the power of the former Roman Empire, while the Vatican City serves as the spiritual heart of the Catholic Church.

Best Time April — October
Currency Euro (€)
Language Italian

Must Visit

  • The Colosseum: The largest ancient amphitheater ever built, an iconic symbol of Imperial Rome.
  • Vatican Museums & Sistine Chapel: Home to some of the most famous art in the world, including Michelangelo's ceiling.
  • The Pantheon: A former Roman temple, now a church, famous for its massive concrete dome and oculus.
  • Trevi Fountain: A Baroque masterpiece where tossing a coin over your shoulder ensures your return to Rome.

The Taste of Rome

Experience authentic Pasta Carbonara (no cream allowed!), try the crispy Pizza al Taglio, and finish every meal with a scoop of artisanal Gelato near the Spanish Steps.
